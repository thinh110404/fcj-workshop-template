# Report on: “Saturday Meet Up: Learn, Share & Connect”

### Event Objectives

- Strengthening practical cloud skills: The event offered a realistic perspective on the journey to mastering cloud certifications and challenges such as AWS Cloud Quest, helping participants clearly envision a structured learning path and develop cloud skills in a solid, practical way rather than relying on theory alone.
- Healing and boosting confidence: Beyond technical content, the event created space to share common psychological barriers in the technology field, such as fear of speaking up, lack of confidence, or Imposter Syndrome, encouraging participants to express themselves more boldly.
- Encouraging a competitive spirit and learning through hands-on experience: Through insights shared by the Hackathon team, the event inspired participants to apply knowledge to real products and projects within limited timeframes, as well as to work effectively in teams under pressure.
- Expanding perspectives on technology products for real life: The introduction of tuvidaiviet.com showed another direction for technology—applying it to spiritual and cultural life, such as fortune-telling, which gave participants a broader view of building technology products for niche markets.

### Key Highlights

#### The journey to mastering AWS Cloud Quest

- Learning cloud through experience: AWS Cloud Quest was presented as a visual and gamified way to learn AWS cloud services, making it easier for beginners to approach than reading dry documentation.
- Building practical skill development paths: The event emphasized the importance of hands-on practice through labs and challenges rather than relying only on theory, in order to understand how cloud infrastructure really works in practice.

#### Overcoming fear of speaking and Imposter Syndrome

- Recognizing Impostor Syndrome: The discussion highlighted a common feeling in the tech world—feeling not good enough, being afraid of being judged when sharing ideas or knowledge in public.
- Building confidence step by step: The event inspired participants to step out of their comfort zones, practice speaking up in small meetings or communities before moving to larger stages.

#### Hands-on experience from the Hackathon

- Time pressure and team spirit: The Hackathon team shared how they worked in groups under tight deadlines and how they divided tasks effectively to complete the product.
- Lessons learned from competition: The team also discussed real challenges encountered while developing a product in a short time and how they handled issues as they arose.

#### Introducing a technology product for real-world use: tuvidaiviet.com

- Applying technology to cultural and spiritual life: tuvidaiviet.com was introduced as a technology product that serves users’ needs for fortune-telling and related cultural-spiritual information through a modern digital platform.
- A niche product perspective: The example showed the opportunity to build technology products for niche markets rather than competing directly in mainstream tech areas.

### What I Learned

#### Personal Development Mindset

- Confidence comes from practice: Speaking up and sharing ideas is a skill that can be developed gradually, not something people are born with.
- Learning should go hand in hand with action: From the AWS Cloud Quest story, learning by doing helps knowledge stick better and be applied more effectively than memorizing theory.

#### Teamwork & Product Skills

- Working in a team under pressure: From the Hackathon experience, I gained a deeper understanding of the importance of clear communication and fair task distribution when time is limited.
- Diverse product thinking: I realized that technology can be applied in many fields beyond traditional tech areas, as shown by tuvidaiviet.com.

### Applying to Work

- Actively practice cloud skills through hands-on learning: Apply the learning-by-doing approach of AWS Cloud Quest to personal skill development.
- Build confidence when sharing ideas: Practice speaking up in team meetings and contributing more often instead of staying silent out of fear of making mistakes.
- Improve teamwork under pressure: Apply lessons from the Hackathon team to real projects with tight deadlines.
- Broaden thinking about technology products: Consider applying technology to niche fields rather than focusing only on mainstream areas.

### Event Experience

> After taking part in the event, I learned not only about Cloud but also about soft skills and confidence. The discussion about the fear of speaking up made me realize that I too sometimes hesitate to share ideas because I fear being wrong. In addition, the experience from the Hackathon and AWS Cloud Quest gave me more motivation to learn through hands-on practice. Overall, this was a very meaningful sharing session that offered practical perspectives and motivation for me to continue learning and growing.
